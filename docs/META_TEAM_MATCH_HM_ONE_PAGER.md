# Meta HM One-Pager

Use this during the hiring manager call.

## What this team likely is

- **ML feature infrastructure for ads ranking**
- Not pure model research
- Not necessarily low-level inference/runtime work
- Likely focused on **feature freshness, online/offline consistency, latency, reliability, cost, and platform adoption**

## What I should optimize for

I want:

- a **named area of ownership**
- **visible metrics**
- **cross-team influence**
- room to grow from **E5 -> E6**

I do **not** want:

- support-heavy work
- diffuse ownership
- hidden plumbing with no attribution
- “help everyone” scope with no decision rights

## My honest framing

> I’m newer to ML infra specifically, but my read is this role is about building a trustworthy feature data plane for ranking: freshness, consistency, observability, latency, and cost. That’s the lens I’m using to ramp.

## Top questions to ask

1. **What exact area would I own in the first 6–12 months?**
2. **What are the top 3 metrics this team is judged on?**
3. **What kind of project here puts someone on an E6 trajectory?**
4. **How do you make sure infra work gets recognized and not hidden behind partner teams?**
5. **How much of the roadmap is proactive platform strategy vs reactive partner requests?**
6. **How much is Python vs C++ in practice?**
7. **What are the biggest technical pain points today: freshness, skew, latency, cost, reliability, or adoption?**
8. **Are LLMs used mainly for offline enrichment, online inference, or internal tooling?**

## What strong answers sound like

- Clear ownership of a **platform surface**
- Metrics like **freshness, p99, cost, adoption, launch speed, quality lift**
- Recent examples of **E5 scope** and **E6 growth**
- Explicit mechanisms for **visibility and attribution**
- Team can **set standards / drive migrations**, not just support others

## Red flags

- “You’ll work across many things.”
- “We collaborate with everyone.”
- “Impact is mainly helping partner teams.”
- “Good work gets noticed naturally.”
- “E6 depends on getting the right opportunity.”

## How to interpret LLM usage

- **Offline enrichment** -> strongest match for feature infra
- **Online inference** -> more latency/perf/C++ heavy
- **Internal tooling** -> more AI productivity/platform

## Best closing question

> If I joined and did really well, what specific kind of project here would make you say I’m on an E6 trajectory?
