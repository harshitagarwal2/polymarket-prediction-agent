## 2. Fastest possible summary

This team likely sits in **ML feature infrastructure for ads ranking**.

That usually means problems like:

- feature freshness
- online/offline consistency
- training-serving skew
- realtime aggregations
- sequence/graph features
- latency / reliability / cost tradeoffs
- platform adoption by ML teams

The role is strongest if you get:

- a **named platform surface**
- **clear metrics**
- **cross-team influence**
- a credible **E5 -> E6** growth path

The role is weaker if it is mostly:

- support work
- migrations with little ownership
- hidden plumbing
- broad collaboration without real decision rights

---

## 3. Your best positioning

Do **not** pretend to already be an ML infra specialist.

Your strongest honest positioning is:

> I’m newer to ML infra specifically, but I’m strong in adjacent platform and distributed systems work. My read is that this role is really about building a trustworthy feature data plane for ranking — freshness, consistency, observability, latency, and cost — and that’s the layer I’ve been ramping on.

That framing is strong because it is:

- honest
- relevant
- systems-oriented
- aligned with the likely role shape

## 4. 30-second recruiter reply draft

> Thanks for reaching out. The team sounds interesting, especially the focus on feature scalability, sequence learning, freshness, and the platform layer for ranking systems. I’m coming from strong adjacent platform and distributed systems work, and I’d love to learn more about the role shape, ownership, and how the team works with ML partners.

---

## 5. 60-second hiring manager intro

> Thanks again for taking the time. I’m newer to ML infra directly, but I’ve spent a lot of time in adjacent platform work where the hard problems were scale, reliability, consistency, observability, and internal-user workflows. What makes this team interesting to me is that my current understanding is that the hard part here is building a reliable feature platform for ranking at scale — freshness, online/offline consistency, latency, and cost — not just model theory in isolation. I’d love to understand how you think about the team’s charter, what this role would own, and what strong impact looks like in the first 6 to 12 months.

---

## 6. Top questions to ask the HM

1. **What exact area would I own in the first 6–12 months?**
2. **What are the top 3 metrics this team is judged on?**
3. **What kind of project here puts someone on an E6 trajectory?**
4. **How do you make sure infra work gets recognized and not hidden behind partner teams?**
5. **How much of the roadmap is proactive platform strategy vs reactive partner requests?**
6. **What are the biggest technical pain points today: freshness, skew, latency, cost, reliability, or adoption?**
7. **How much is Python vs C++ in practice?**
8. **Are LLMs used mainly for offline enrichment, online inference, or internal tooling?**

---

## 7. What good answers sound like

- clear platform ownership
- visible metrics
- recent E5 scope and E6 growth examples
- explicit attribution for infra work
- proactive roadmap control
- realistic ramp for someone coming from adjacent platform work

## 8. Red flags

- “You’ll work across many things.”
- “We collaborate with everyone.”
- “Impact is mainly helping partner teams.”
- “Good work gets noticed naturally.”
- “E6 depends on the right opportunity.”

---

## 9. If you only remember one thing

Your main job in the HM call is to figure out:

> Is this a high-influence seat on a high-influence team, or a support-heavy seat on an important team?

That is the key difference between a strong E5 growth opportunity and a vague one.
